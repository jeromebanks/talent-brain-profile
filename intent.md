# Career Intent & Preferences

## Most Important Factors

1. Involves AI/emerging tech in a real way — close to a hard requirement; would pass up a higher-paying role that was pure maintenance or contract piece-work without it
2. Technically challenging work that leverages data engineering and distributed systems skills, ideally at large scale ("big data")
3. High autonomy — ownership of large areas of a system — within a collaborative, apolitical, small team of smart people; not a silo, not command-and-control, but still disciplined about engineering best practices
4. Fast-paced but disciplined early-to-mid stage startup, or an equivalent small team inside a larger technical organization
5. Compensation at SF Bay Area market rate, sufficient to keep saving for retirement; open to trading some base for startup equity upside, but not as a substitute for the technical/mission fit

Biotech is an ideal domain given my data engineering and distributed systems background, but any domain is fine if it involves AI and large-scale data.

Day to day, I want to be working at the intersection of AI/ML and large-scale data engineering, on a team that measures my value by what I deliver.

Targeting Staff+ scope as a hands-on individual contributor — not interested in people management.

Large, bureaucratic organizations with heavy internal politics. Also roles where the mandate is to change how a team works or behaves without real authority or buy-in — being a "force multiplier" is fine, but I want a role where my value is measured by my deliverables, not primarily by how well I can influence people who aren't asking to be influenced.

Adtech is fine as a domain but not exciting — I've done it several times already and would rather explore something new; otherwise no industry is off the table. I'm pragmatic on tech stack with no strong ecosystem allegiance.
